Paratha

Parathas or Paranthas come under the category of Indian breads which are commonly consumed as breakfast in north India. It is made by pan frying whole wheat dough on a tava. The parantha dough usually contains ghee or cooking oil which is also layered on the freshly prepared paratha. Paranthas are usually stuffed with boiled potatoes (as in aloo ka parantha), leaf vegetables, radishes, cauliflower, and/or paneer. A parantha (especially a stuffed one) can be eaten simply with a pat of butter spread on top, with chutney, pickles, and yogurt, or with meat or vegetable curries.

Idli

Idli is a traditional breakfast in south Indian households. Idli is a savory cake of South India that is very popular throughout India. The cakes are usually two to three inches in diameter and are made by steaming a batter consisting of fermented black lentils (de-husked) and rice. The fermentation process breaks down the starches so that they are more readily metabolized by the body. Most often eaten at breakfast or as a snack, idlis are usually served in pairs with chutney, sambar, or other accompaniments. Mixtures of crushed dry spices such as milagai podi are the preferred condiment for idlis eaten on the go.

Masala Dosa

Dosa also called Chatamari in Newari, is a fermented crepe or pancake made from rice batter and black lentils. This staple dish is widely popular in all southern Indian states Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu and Kerala, as well as being popular in other countries like Sri Lanka and Singapore. Dosa, a common breakfast dish and street food, is rich in carbohydrates, and contains no sugar or saturated fats. Since its constituent ingredients are rice and lentils, it is gluten-free and contains protein. The fermentation process increases the vitamin B and vitamin C content. There are also instant mix products for making dosa, with somewhat lower nutritional benefits.

A masala dosa is made by stuffing a dosa with a lightly cooked filling of potatoes, fried onions and spices. The dosa is wrapped around an onion and potato curry or sabji. In Bangalore, the masala dosa is usually served with a red chutney applied to its inside surface. The red chutney usually has generous amounts of garlic.

Paneer Tikka

Paneer tikka is an Indian dish made from chunks of paneer marinated in spices and grilled in a tandoor. It is a vegetarian alternative to chicken tikka and other meat dishes. It is a popular dish that is widely available in India and other countries with an Indian diaspora. Chunks of paneer, a type of fresh cheese, are marinated in spices and are then arranged on a stick with capsicums, onions and tomatoes. These sticks are grilled in a tandoor and the dish is thereafter served hot, seasoned with lemon juice and chaat masala. It is sometimes accompanied by salad or mint chutney. Tikka dishes traditionally go well with mint chutney. The paneer, though tender, has a crisp singe on the surface.

Vegetable Cutlet

The vegetable cutlet contains a rich filling of the combination of mashed potatoes, onion, green chillies, spices and salt, cooked for a bit together. The cutlet can be either pan fried or deep fried. It is also consumed as a type of evening snack. This is more popular with the dominantly vegetarian Indian population. An example is the Aloo Tikki.

Corn Cheese Balls

The corn cheese balls although of american origin is a famous food item in Indian restaurants. It is a mixture of cheese, sweet corn, beans, carrots, onions, spices, corriander and other vegetables which are bound together by corn flour. These round balls are deep fried is served with green chutney and it also goes well with tomato ketchup.

Kadhai Paneer

Kadhai Paneer has an irresistable flavor with the blend of green bell peppers, dried fenugreek leaves and cardamom. Its very healthy and nutritious dish as it consumes very less fat and no cream. This food is very popular in North India. In the image, Paneer and colorful bell peppers are sauteed in spicy tomato gravy.

Bhindi Masala

Bhindi is the hindi word for Lady's finger. Bhindi Masala is a popular main course dish with bhindi cooked in spices and served with chapatis and rotis. The food is cooked by stuffing bhindi with ingredients and the spicy flavors spread over entire bhindi making the dish delicious.

Vegetable Pulao

Vegetable Pulao or Pilaf is a rice dish mixed with vegetables and mildly flavored with the spices like Cardamom, Cloves and Cinnamon to give it an aromatic flavor. Vegetable Pulao is a wonderful one pot meal which is cooked with a blend of spices and vegetables with flavorful Basmati rice.

Gaajar ka Halwa

Gajar Ka Halwa sometimes known as Gajrela, Carrot Halwa or gajar ka gajerela is a sweet dessert pudding associated mainly with the state of Punjab in India & Pakistan. It is made by placing grated carrot in a jar containing a specific amount of water, milk and sugar and then cooked, stirring regularly. It is often served with a garnish of almonds and pistachios. The nuts and other items used are first sautéed in ghee, a South Asian clarified butter. Gajar ka Halwa is described as a Halva having a combination of nuts, milk, sugar and ghee with grated carrot. It is a light Punjabi dessert with less fat (a minimum of 10.03% and an average of 12.19%), so it is treated as a light nutritious dessert. Gajar halwa has a medium shelf life so it is now sometimes exported.

Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a popular cheese based dessert similar to dumpling. It is made mainly from milk solids, traditionally from freshly curdled milk. These milks solids, known as khoya in India, are kneaded into a dough, sometimes with a pinch of flour, and then shaped into small balls and deep fried at a low temperature. The balls are then soaked in a light sugar syrup flavored with green cardamom and rosewater, kewra or saffron.

Rasmalai

Rasmalai is a sweet dessert originating in India. Is served after a meal. The name Ras Malai comes from two parts in Hindi: "Ras" meaning "juice/juicy" and "Malai", meaning "cream". Ras Malai consists of sugary white, cream or yellow coloured balls (or flattened balls) of paneer soaked in malai (clotted cream) flavoured with cardamom.[2] Homemade Ras Malai is usually made up of powdered milk, all-purpose flour, baking powder and oil; kneaded to form a dough, moulded into balls and dropped into simmering milk cream.

Chicken Cutlet

In the culinary arts, the term Cutlet is used to refer to a thin cut of meat usually taken from the leg or rib section of veal, pork or lamb. Chicken cutlets can be made from a thinly sliced and pounded chicken breast. Cutlets are typically pan-fried, usually after dredging in flour and/or coating them in breadcrumbs. In Indian cuisine, a cutlet specifically refers to cooked meat (mutton, beef, fish or chicken) stuffing that is fried with a batter/covering. The meat itself is cooked with spices - onion, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, coriander (cilantro), green chillies, lemon and salt. This is then dipped in an egg mix and then in breadcrumbs, and fried in ghee or vegetable oil.

Chicken Kaati Roll

These chicken kathi rolls make for a great snack or party food. Kathi roll also known as Kati Roll is a street-food originating from Kolkata, India. Its original form was a kati kabab enclosed in a paratha, but over the years many variants have evolved all of which now go under the generic name of Kati Roll. Today, pretty much any filling rolled up in any kind of Indian flatbread is called a kati roll. The innovations tend to be in two areas – the fillings or the wrap. Common variants on the filling are egg, potato, paneer, mixed vegetables and curried chicken or mutton. More exotic versions can have combinations of ingredients, or fancy curries such as Thai or Schezwan.

Egg Pakora

Pakora is a fried snack (fritter) found across South Asia. Pakoras are created by taking one or two ingredients such as onion, eggplant, potato, spinach, plantain, paneer, cauliflower, tomato, chili pepper, bread, egg or chicken and dipping them in a batter of gram flour and then deep-frying them. The most popular varieties of veg pakoras are palak pakora, made from spinach, paneer pakora, made from paneer. In southern states of India, such preparations are known as bajji rather than pakora. Usually the name of the item that is deep fried is suffixed with bajji. For instance, potato bajji is sliced potato wrapped in batter and deep fried. In such states, pakoda is taken to mean a mix of finely cut onions, green chillies and spices mixed in gram flour. This is rolled into small balls or sprinkled straight in hot oil and fried. These pakodas are very crisp on the outside and medium soft to crisp inside. Egg pakoras are egg dipped into the batter and deep fried. It is a great evening snack.

Chicken Kabab

Kabab refers to meat that is cooked over or next to flames. It can be large or small cuts of meat or even ground meat. In India the distinct flavor of kabab is specially due to the added spices. Chicken is marinated with all the spices so that the taste is absorbed by the meat. It is then either grilled or deep fried. It is a very popular non-veg starter that is consumed all over India.

Fish Fry

A fish fry is a meal containing battered or breaded fried fish. One of the main items in a complete South Indian seafood meal is fish fry. The use of spices adds great taste to it. The fish is marinated so that the taste of the masala soaks into it and later after applying rava, it is either pan fried or deep oil fried.

Fried Prawns

Shrimp and prawn are important types of seafood that are consumed worldwide. The prawn is coated with flour, beaten egg and breadcrumbs in that order, and deep-fried in hot cooking oil. The prawn tastes better when fried using olive oil. The Indian version of the prawn fry is spicy and is usually preferred as a side dish for a meal.

Chicken Biryani

Biryani is a rice-based dish made with spices, rice (usually basmati rice) and chicken or mutton. The name is derived from the Persian word beryā which means "fried" or "roasted". Biryani was believed to have been invented in the kitchen of Mughal Emperors. It is very popular in the Indian subcontinent and is a key element of the South Asian cuisine. Hyderabad, Malabar, Delhi/Agra, Kashmir, Kolkata, Lucknow are the main centres of biryani cuisine. The chicken is marinated in lemon juice, yoghurt, ginger, garlic, salt, turmeric powder, cumin powder, tomato purée. The variety of ingredients that are used during preparation biryani delicious. It is usually served with raitha or plain curds.

Murgh Makhani

Also known as Butter Chicken, this rich, buttery, creamy chicken needs no introduction. Most probably the best known of all Indian dishes, it has already claimed the fame that it deserves. Butter chicken is a classic Indian recipe that originated in Punjab, India. Chicken (with or without bones) is marinated overnight in a yogurt and spice mixture usually including garam masala, ginger, garlic paste, lemon or lime, pepper, coriander, cumin, turmeric and chili. The chicken is traditionally cooked in a tandoor, but can also be grilled, roasted or pan fried. The sauce for Butter Chicken is made with pureed juicy tomatoes, and spices cooked in butter, and is finished off with fresh cream. The primary aroma of the dish unfolds with the addition of the kasuri methi/dried fenugreek leaves. Butter chicken is usually served with naan, roti, parathas, roomali roti or steamed rice.

Fish Curry

Curry is the generic English term primarily employed in Western culture to denote a wide variety of dishes whose origins are in Indian cuisine and Southeast Asian cuisines. Traditionally, spices are used both whole and ground; cooked or raw; and they may be added at different times during the cooking process to produce different results. Dishes called "curry" may contain meat, poultry, fish, or shellfish, either alone or in combination with vegetables. Fish curry is a blend of flavours distilled from Chinese and Portuguese origin. Curry blends in the rich flavours of authentic spices, such as coriander, turmeric, cumin, fenugreek and red pepper and accumulates those dense flavours into the meat or fish and the corresponding gravy. They are consumed with rice.
